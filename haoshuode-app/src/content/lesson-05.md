```vocab
wǒ | I, me, we | | 我
nánrén | man, male | | 男人
nǐ | you | | 你
qún | community, group | | 群
xīn | new, another, fresh | | 新
```

Hao-shuo-de pronouns behave like any other noun you've already met — <audio-example zh="我">wǒ</audio-example> ("I") and <audio-example zh="你">nǐ</audio-example> ("you") simply take the Subject spot from Lesson 3, no special treatment required.

And like every Hao-shuo-de noun, they carry no number of their own. <audio-example zh="我">wǒ</audio-example> means "I" or "we" depending on what's already clear from the situation — recall Lesson 2: nothing in the word marks the difference, only the context around it does.

To show possession, bind the pronoun to a noun with `-de`, the same connecting particle from Lessons 2 and 4: <audio-example zh="我的">wǒ-de</audio-example> ("my"), <audio-example zh="你的">nǐ-de</audio-example> ("your"). Same hyphen, same job — gluing one word onto another to form a single descriptive unit — whether what's doing the describing is an adjective, a verb turned into a noun, or now, a pronoun.

```examples
Wǒ shì rén. | I am a person / We are people. | | 我是人。
Nǐ shì hǎo-de rén. | You are a good person. | | 你是好的人。
Zhè-ge shì wǒ-de xiě-de dōngxi. | This is my document. | | 这个是我的写的东西。
Nǐ-de dìfāng hěn xīn. | Your place is new. | | 你的地方很新。
Wǒ-de qún hěn dà. | My community is large. | | 我的群很大。
Nánrén-de dòngwù hěn xiǎo. | The man's animal is small. | | 男人的动物很小。
```

